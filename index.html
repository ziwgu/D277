History of Nevada

Nevada, also known as the "Silver State," derived its name from its deeply rooted mining history. Nevada was originally inhabited by indigenous groups such as the Paiute, Shoshone, and Washoe tribes before 1859, when the Comstock Lode was discovered. This led to a large mining rush that resulted in Nevada's statehood (State of Nevada Joint Information Center).

Today, Nevada has moved forward to become a state of natural beauty, historical significance, and modern attractions that include many festivals and industries such as casinos.

Nevada Map

Here is a map of Nevada. As you can see, Nevada is made up of 17 counties. The majority of the population within Nevada lives on the western and southern regions primarily within Las Vegas, Reno, and Carson City.

The surrounding states are California, Oregon, Idaho, Utah, and Arizona.

Nevada has many large bodies of water including Lake Tahoe, Pyramid Lake, Angel Lake, Lake Las Vegas, and more.
