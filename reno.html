Welcome To Reno

This is the official page for Reno, Nevada by the Nevada Government Office. This page is dedicated to helping tourists and residents explore what the "Biggest Little City" has to show for itself.

History of Reno

Reno was founded in 1868 and, similar to many other popular cities in Nevada, was also a hub for mining and railroads. It first gained traction in the 20th century as a center for gambling, quickly earning its title of "The Biggest Little City in the World."

Today, Reno is holding true to its roots in gambling and proximity to historical sites and Lake Tahoe.

Fun Facts About Reno

  1. The city population is 264,165 as of 2020 (United States Census Bureau)
  2. Reno was officially incorporated into Nevada in 1903 (Nevada Historical Society)
  3. Reno is part of Washoe county and is on the western region of the state
  4. Reno as one of Nevada's fastest growing cities, is very urban and expected to grow even faster
  5. Reno has an median household income of $72,103 compared to the state median of $72,333 (United States Census Bureau)
