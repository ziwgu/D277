History of Carson City

Carson City was founded in 1858 and was named after the famous frontiersman Kit Carson. During the silver rush of Nevada's early history, the site that would be Carson City was mainly a trading post and mining hub, but with growing residence and growth, it became the center of government and commerce.

Carson City remains Nevada's capital and continues to be the city that upholds the state government and serves as a cultural hub to explore the history of the state.

Fun Facts About Carson City

  • The city population is 58,639 as of 2022 (United States Census Bureau)
  • Carson City was officially incorporated into Nevada in 1875 (Visit Carson City, 2024)
  • Carson City used to be apart of Ormsby county on the western region of the state (Visit Carson City, 2024)
  • Despite being relatively low in population compared to Reno and Las Vegas, Carson City is still considered an urban city
  • Carson City has a median household income of $67,465 compared to the state median of $72,333 (United States Census Bureau)
